# Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Short, straight answers to the questions we hear most. If you don't find what you need here, reach out to support.

## What is Bitnex?

Bitnex (bitnex.pro) is a non-custodial trading interface for perpetual futures (perps) and spot markets, built on top of Hyperliquid, a decentralized on-chain exchange. Bitnex is the front-end you trade through; Hyperliquid provides the liquidity, the matching engine and the custody of funds. You get a clean, fast trading experience with a simple "Lite" view and a full "Pro" view, plus Portfolio, Vaults/Earn, Staking, Referrals and Swap.

## Is Bitnex custodial? Does Bitnex hold my funds?

No. Bitnex is fully non-custodial. Your funds never leave your control and are never held by Bitnex. They live in Hyperliquid's on-chain system, where you remain in control at all times.

## Do I need to sign up or complete KYC?

There is no traditional sign-up and no KYC on Bitnex itself. You get started by connecting a crypto wallet (MetaMask, Coinbase Wallet, or Rainbow) or by logging in with email or Google, which creates an embedded wallet for you via Privy.

## What are the fees?

Bitnex charges a small fee on top of Hyperliquid's standard trading fees. The exact fee is always shown in the Order Details before you place a trade, so you can review it every time before confirming.

## What networks or chains does Bitnex use?

You deposit USDC by bridging from Arbitrum to Hyperliquid, directly from inside the app. Withdrawals go back to Arbitrum. Trading itself happens on Hyperliquid's on-chain system.

## What is the "agent wallet," and why do I sign to "Enable Trading"?

When you enable trading, you approve one-time signatures that create an "agent wallet" (a session key). This lets you place orders without a wallet pop-up on every single trade, so trading is gasless once set up. These approvals are signed on the Arbitrum network.

{% hint style="info" %}
The agent key **cannot withdraw your funds**, and you can revoke it at any time.
{% endhint %}

## Is Bitnex safe? Can Bitnex withdraw my funds?

Bitnex cannot withdraw your funds. Because Bitnex is non-custodial, your funds stay in Hyperliquid's on-chain system under your control. The agent wallet used for trading can place orders but cannot move funds out, and it can be revoked whenever you choose.

## Who can use Bitnex?

Bitnex is **not available to U.S. persons** or to persons located in restricted or sanctioned jurisdictions. Access is geo-restricted, and on your first connection you must confirm by signing that you are not a U.S. person.

{% hint style="warning" %}
If you are a U.S. person or located in a restricted or sanctioned jurisdiction, you are not eligible to use Bitnex.
{% endhint %}

## Do I have to accept Terms of Service?

Yes. On your first connection, you must accept the Terms of Service by signing a message with your wallet. This signature is recorded for compliance. The current Terms version is 1.0.0.

## How do I deposit and withdraw?

Deposits are made by bridging USDC from Arbitrum to Hyperliquid, right inside the app. When you want to take funds out, you withdraw back to Arbitrum. You stay in control of your funds throughout.

## What is leverage, and what is the risk?

Leverage lets you open a perps position larger than the collateral you put up, which increases both potential gains and potential losses. Trading with leverage carries real risk, including the possibility of losing your funds. Only trade with what you can afford to lose, and make sure you understand how a position can be liquidated before you use leverage.

{% hint style="danger" %}
Leveraged trading can lead to the loss of your funds. Understand the risks before you trade.
{% endhint %}
